Mei Lenehan

Mei Lenehan is a Junior in Graphic Design at RISD. She believes in the words and disagrees with the fetishization of form for form’s sake—for now. She runs a bi-annual open format publication called YTB, is a designer for RISD’s Design Guild, writes poetry and short stories, has a cat named Leroy, a roommate named Anna , an instagram, and a very good friend Tim who made this website.

2016 Block Party Poster

Poster for the Rhode Island School of Design’s annual block party club fair. Type set in Plantin Titling.

2016 Type as Form but also Still as Type

Single letter, letter size compositions exploiting indesign’s drop shadow effect for Micah Barrett’s Type III class. There weren’t many guidelines to this exercise besides to have fun and embrace spontinaety :-)

2016 RISD Gallery Exhibition Schedule

A project that began for Micah Barrett’s Type III course in the form of a large poster, smaller individual event flyers, and a gif. The project was extended out of class into “reality” as exhibition schedule cards for RISD’s Woods-Gerry and Sol Koffler galleries.

2016 Two Stones Three Ways

Two 8 page publications as the first iterations of Paul Soulellis’ Archive Project in Design Studio 3. We were asked to use an existing archive to create narrative. Using a classroom displays Flickr pool I became most interested in how the attitude and intent of making can influence its outcome.

2015 Cars and Type Banner

A celebratory banner illustrating similarities between typographic and automotive forms for Ernesto Aparicio’s Type I course.
